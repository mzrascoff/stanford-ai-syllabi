How this was made

Course listings and syllabi for Spring 2026 were collected from Stanford's official Syllabus tool, supplemented by syllabi that instructors publish as shared Google Docs. Of courses offered, had a syllabus available; had machine-readable text and form the basis of this analysis. Each syllabus was scanned for any reference to AI or generative-AI tools, and the relevant passage was classified into one of five patterns by language model, then spot-checked against the source wording shown in the table.

Read these numbers carefully. The tiers reflect what each syllabus says, not how instructors apply policies day to day. Some syllabi are posted only as restricted links or images and cannot be read; those are excluded. About a fifth of uploaded files could not be matched to a course code but are still counted in totals. This is a descriptive snapshot, not an official Stanford position.

Source: Stanford Syllabus tool (syllabus.stanford.edu) and instructor Google Docs, Spring 2026 term · retrieved . Classifications are automated and approximate. Not affiliated with or endorsed by Stanford University.
