How this was made

Syllabi for each quarter were collected from Stanford's central Syllabus tool (plus instructor-shared docs), their text extracted, scanned for any mention of AI/generative-AI, and the relevant passage classified into five patterns by a language model — then spot-checked against the source wording shown in each row. The "evidence" column lets you audit every call.

Read carefully. Tiers reflect what a syllabus says, not classroom practice. Image-only or unreadable syllabi are excluded, so each quarter's "analyzed" count is the readable subset (Fall and Winter had more image-only syllabi than Spring, so their totals are lower — compare the percentages, which are coverage-robust). Automated and approximate; not an official Stanford position, and not affiliated with or endorsed by Stanford University.
